Co-Editor-in-Chief

Xinhuan Zeng (曾芯焕)

Hanyang University

Xinhuan Zeng is a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Dance at Hanyang University. His pioneering research explores the intersection of artificial intelligence and dance science, specifically focusing on the visualization and analysis of dance movements (such as Chinese classical dance and ballet) using advanced AI technologies like OpenPose and Mediapipe.

By combining AI motion tracking with expert evaluation models, his work aims to uncover the cognitive processes behind dance learning and creative decision-making. He also possesses extensive experience in choreography and digital interactive performance, having represented international teams at events such as the Daegu International Dance Festival.

He serves as the Co-Editor-in-Chief of the Global Review of Humanities, Arts and Society (GRHAS), where he drives international academic exchange and oversees rigorous editorial management.
