Editor-in-Chief

Qilun Hu

Kookmin University & Nanjing Communication University

Qilun Hu is a faculty member in Broadcasting and Hosting Arts at Nanjing Communication University, and is currently pursuing a doctoral degree in Journalism and Communication at Kookmin University, South Korea, through a combined master-doctoral program. He holds a bachelor’s degree in Broadcasting and Hosting Arts from Nanjing University of the Arts.

His primary research focuses on political communication, AIGC (AI-Generated Content), intelligent communication, and new media. He has a particular academic interest in the socio-political implications of algorithmic recommendation systems, political polarization, information pollution, and echo chambers in modern digital ecosystems.

He serves in key leadership roles within academic publishing, currently holding the position of Editor-in-Chief for the Journal of Law, Psychology, and Communication Studies and Associate Editor for the Global Review of Humanities, Arts, and Society.
