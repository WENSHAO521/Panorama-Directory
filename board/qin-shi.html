Qin Shi

Panorama Scholarly Group

Qin Shi is a distinguished researcher and choreographer specializing in dance arts. He holds a Ph.D. in Arts (Dance) from Kangwon National University, South Korea, alongside a Master's degree in Ethnic Folk Dance Performance and a Bachelor's degree in Dance Education from the Shenyang Conservatory of Music.

From 2020 to 2023, he served as a full-time Lecturer in Dance at Chongqing Institute of Foreign Trade and Economics. His academic and artistic research focuses on the cultural inheritance, pedagogical methods, and digital preservation of Chinese ethnic and folk dances, particularly Tibetan, Qiang, and Yi dance traditions. He is a core participant in several prestigious research initiatives, including the National Arts Fund project and the Ministry of Education Youth Project exploring multimodal knowledge graphs for intangible cultural heritage dances.

As an acclaimed choreographer and educator, he has received numerous international and national accolades, including the Choreography Award at the KACIEA International Dance Competition, the Grand Prize from the South Korean National Assembly’s Culture, Sports and Tourism Committee, and multiple "Outstanding Instructor" honors. He currently serves as an Editorial Board Member for the Global Review of Humanities, Arts, and Society.
